AGAR has completed first-person interviews and documentaries about attending, being a parent, or teaching at Amherst County Public Schools from the early 20th-century through 1975. There are 57 first-person interviews now archives and available. The project was partially funded by the Virginia Foundation for the Humanities (VFH), 2013-2015 and the humanities advisor was Dr. Lynn Rainville. The 57 interviews were transferred to DVDs and 38 were transcribed. AGAR produced 21 short documentary videos. For a list of schools discussed in the DVDs, click here. The DVDs have been archived with the VFH and the Amherst County Museum and Historical Society.

The current project built upon two previous AGAR interview and documentary projects, "African-American Schools in Amherst County and Early Integration" and "Three Amherst Schools: Amherst County Training School, Bear Mountain Indian Mission School and Clifford School," also archived at VFH and ACM. Please contact AGAR with inquiries about the project: Telephone, 434-989-3215 or email the project director, Lynn Kable.

To learn more about each of these segregation-era schools, click on the photos above. The Amherst Glebe Arts Response has recently released a documentary featuring the remembrances of school alumni. The 70-minute DVD (produced by the Amherst Glebe Arts Response) is available for purchase ($12, the money raised goes to a local scholarship fund).
