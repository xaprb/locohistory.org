The following twenty-one documentary films have been created on seven DVDS to tell the stories of the schools studied in the words of those who taught at or attended or were parents at them. Copies have been given to the Amherst County Museum and the VFH:

Disc One
            1. Mt. Pleasant School
            2. Temperance Schools
            3. Oak Grove School at Wright Shop Road  (AKA Wright Shop Road School)

Disc Two
            4. Old Pleasant View School
            5. New Pleasant View School

Disc Three
            6. Old Elon School
            7. Monroe Schools
            8. Madison Heights High School / Phelps Road

Disc Four
              9. Sandidges School
            10. Bellevue School
            11. Lowesville Schools
            12. Madison Heights Elementary School / Phelps Road
            13. New Elon School (opened 1968)
            14. Pedlar Mills School

Disc Five
            15. Amherst Elementary (1918-62) and High Schools (prior to 1956) and Early Days at Amherst County High School (1956-62)
            16. More Stories from Amherst Elementary & High School and Amherst County High School

Disc Six
            17. Old Amherst Elementary School
            18. Amherst Academy (Private School from time of Integration)

Disc Seven

            19. Integration of Amherst County Public Schools
            20. Oak Grove School in Pedlar District
            21. Edgewood School at Pera.

AGAR interviewers finally included Sandra Esposito, Edward Kable, Lynn Kable, Kathryn Pixley, Kitty Swanson with outreach assistance from Beverly Keith Campbell,
Beverly Campbell Jones, Jean Higginbotham and John Gatewood. Interviewers received training from Donovan Media Center and/or Lynn Rainville, PhD.

Videographer:
Ned Kable was videographer for all project interviews, and copied the individual interviews onto DVDs. Individual interviews appear on one DVD (or, in a few cases, two friends, father and son, or a married couple in same interview.) Equipment for this project was purchased through a grant from Greater Lynchburg Community Trust and
Ned Kable was trained in its use by Bill Noel of Donvan Media Center. The videographer for the previous projects in 2009-2012 was Mia Magruder.

Photos and Memorabilia
AGAR received access to memorabilia (especially yearbooks) and photos from Jacqueline Beidler, Mary Brugh, Judith Faris, Louise Faulconer, Leah Settle Gibbs, 
Ella Magruder,  Helen Massie, Florence, Holcomb, and Alan Nixon, Kathryn Pixley, Marita Taylor, Bob and Betty Wimer, and from Amherst County Museum and 
Historical Society, the Amherst County Public Schools and the Amherst County Public Library.

With the help of local historians Florence and Holcomb Nixon and Beverly Keith Campbell, AGAR was able to view and photograph sites, and the remains of several schools no longer extant.  These include Macedonia Church (outhouses still standing) Edgewood at Pera School (still standing) Pearch School (no longer), Oak Grove/Pedlar 
District (church and school fell down, church foundation visible). Pedlar Mills School (went down Pedlar River in hurricane Camille) and from the African-American Schools 
Timothy (near standing Timothy Church, school torn down) and Chestnut Grove Baptist Church (school torn down, church is there).
