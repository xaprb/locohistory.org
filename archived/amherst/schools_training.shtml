Three Schools Project in Amherst County: Amherst County Training School

Amherst Training School, originally called the Amherst Negro School, was built as a six- classroom wooden frame structure on School Street in Amherst, Virginia, in 1921, with contributions from businesses and local families in the Black community. Additional classrooms and two additional buildings, one a kitchen and lunchroom, the second a classroom brick structure, were added over the years.

An October, 1941, Engineers' Report (submitted to the School Board of Amherst County by W.D. Campbell and Sons, Inc.) features a photo of the school with the following description: "Amherst Negro School, Amherst, Virginia. A one story and part basement, frame building with composition shingle roof and wood floors. Wood sealed finish, electric lighting, stove heat.  Approximately 19 years old, in fair condition. Occupancy – six class rooms and kitchen."

Elementary Students who attended the Training School lived primarily within the Town of Amherst.  Black students from one- and two-room elementary schools in the northern half of Amherst County also fed into Amherst Training School for high school. Like other Amherst County public schools at the time, the Training School had no kindergarten and no 12th grade.  The Southern end of the County did not have a Black high school until the Madison Heights School, serving grades one through eleven, opened in 1939.

Central High School, opened in 1956. It served all Black public high school students in Amherst County.  Amherst Training School continued to serve elementary students until Central Elementary School opened as an all-Black consolidated Amherst County elementary School in 1964.  Although Black students began being integrated into Amherst County High School on a very limited basis in 1963, true integration, court ordered and enforced, did not come to Amherst County High School until 1969-70.

Teachers and students interviewed about Amherst Training School:

  • Willie Diggs, retired upholsterer, was a student at Amherst Training School 1939-1947.
  • Hon. Willard H. Douglas Jr., is a retired Judge for Commonwealth of Virginia. He was a student at Amherst Training School, and graduated in the class of 1949.
  • Rev. Jasper Fletcher, retired Amherst Magistrate, was a student at Amherst Training School 1946-1956.
  • Mabel Massie Hughes, now a school bus driver Amherst County School System, was a student at Amherst Training School in grades 6-11. Later she was a parent at Amherst Training School and at Amherst County integrated schools.
  • Mrs. Edith Jones, interviewed for this project in 2009 at age 101, was an elementary teacher at Amherst Training School beginning in the 1950’s.
  • Robert Langstaff is the general manager of WAMV Radio, originally built as a classroom building for Amherst Training School.
  • Pastor Margaret Nelson is a minister, who retired as a Full Colonel from the US Air Force. She was a student at Amherst Training School from 1945-1950.
  • Annie Chambers Pinn, MS, is a retired high school guidance counselor, who taught high school at Amherst Training School 1948-1951.
  • Gilbert Rose, co-owner of What a Blessing Bakery, was a Student at Amherst Training School in the1950s.
  • Patricia Rose, co-owner of What a Blessing Bakery, was a student at Amherst Training School 1954-1961.
  • Jean Higginbotham Sandidge, a retired social work supervisor from Amherst County, was a student at Amherst Training School 1948-1956.
  • Robert Sandidge, landscaper, was a student at Amherst Training School 1954-1962.
  • Helen Williams, Administrative Assistant for over 50 years for the Virginia Tech Virginia Cooperative Extension Service, Amherst Office, was a student at Amherst Training School 1945-1956
