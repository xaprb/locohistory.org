Three Schools Project in Amherst County
Clifford School, Fletcher's Level Road, Clifford, Amherst County

The public school system in Amherst County, Virginia, was set up in the late 19th century.  The Clifford School was built at that time as a frame building replacing a previous private school, and had a similar style and shape to other schools being built then in the County.  It was a T-shaped building housing three classrooms for grades one through six.  The school served the white children of Greater Clifford.  School teachers were housed at 121 Turkey Mountain Road, once the Blacksmith's House.  This school had outdoor toilets and was heated by wood stoves.

The "new" Clifford School opened in 1939. It had four classrooms and a kitchen.  The principalâ€™s office was in one of the classrooms. This was an "improved" building with electricity, central heating, radiators, and bathrooms. The Clifford School continued to serve white children, grades one to seven, from the greater Clifford area until full integration and consolidation of the elementary school system took place. This was court ordered and enforced in Amherst County between 1969-70.

Students, Teachers, Parents and Staff Interviewed / Old and New Clifford Schools

  • Mary Brugh is a retired newspaper proofreader for Amherst New Era Progress, who was a parent at Clifford School 1965-67.
  • Frances Butler is an innovative community volunteer in the greater Lynchburg area, and was a student at Clifford School for six years in the 1940s.
  • Eleanor Harris Caldwell is a retired secretary for Verizon who was a student at Clifford School 1955-1961.
  • Louise Cash is a retired teacher from the Amherst County School system who was a student at Clifford School 1925-1932.
  • Linda Crews was a high school teacher in Nelson County.  She was a student at Clifford School 1959-1967 and attended Amherst County High School at the time of integration.
  • Elizabeth Garland is a retired hotel industry front office worker who was a  student at Clifford School 1925-1931.
  • Richard Hanson is the retired founding owner of Rebec Vineyards. He was an active Parents Association President and a parent at Clifford School for many years.
  • Marie Harris retired as a secretary at Sweet Briar College. She was a parent at Clifford School and worked there as a cook in the 1950s.
  • C. Swanson Jones is the retired owner of George E. Jones Construction Company. He was a student at Clifford School 1930-37
  • Ella Magruder is a professor of dance at Sweet Briar College.  She was a student at Clifford School first through seventh grades and attended Amherst Consolidated High School during the time of integration.
  • Helen Smith Massie is herself a retired teacher, and was the daughter of Clifford School teacher Mrs. Smith.
  • Alice Howell Powell is a retired teacher, who was a student at both the old and new Clifford Schools in grades one through six in the1930s and 1940s.
  • Pat Richeson is the retired assistant to chaplain at Sweet Briar College, who also has worked as a dance teacher and artist. She was a student at Clifford School 1947-1953.
  • Alma Simmons Rountrey is a poet, and retired high school Latin Teacher who was an elementary teacher at the old  Clifford School 1935-1937.
  • Page Stinnett is a retired newspaper typesetter and printer for the Amherst New Era Progress. He was a student at the old Clifford School 1930-1937.
  • Kitty Boxley Swanson is a retired bookkeeper, who was a student at the new Clifford School 1947-1952.
  • Morris Thomas retired from the American Cyanamid plant, and is also a farmer. He was a student at the old Clifford School from the early 1930s through 1938.
